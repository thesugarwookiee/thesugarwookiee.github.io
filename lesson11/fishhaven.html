Looking Forward

Really, how big was that fish, though?

That's the sort of question we need to be asking ourselves. As people of Fish Haven, as we look forward to the coming months and events, we have to remember what our roots are. In this case, it's all about the fish. There's no point in being here if you don't like the fish. Yes, we do fish, and we do eat those little creatures we love, but we do it as ethical people who don't take more than we need. So with the future in mind--with fresh and healthy populations of our favorite critters--we really do need to think about how big a fish is when we keep it. Both the size and the impact it makes on our ecosystem is vital!

It's a bit like a dandelion, really. What seems like an insigificant act now--taking a fish that we weren't meant to take from the lake--can have great repercusions later. When we blow on a puffy dandelion it may delight us for a moment or two, but those seeds go on and sew themselves into our lawns and affect the area for a long time to come. This becomes worse, of course, if we do even more than just the one. One fish becomes two or three or more, much like a child in a field of dandelion puffs.

Ultimately, the point is that what makes us happy in the moment might hurt us or someone else down the line. So before you go and do the childish thing and ignore the requests of local fish officials, make sure you understand the impact that discretion might have on things that are way more important than just feeling good about catching yourself a fish to take home.
